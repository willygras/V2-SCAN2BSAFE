SCAN2BESAFE: BCMA Clinical Simulation Platform
The gap between nursing school and the real ward is where mistakes happen.

SCAN2BESAFE is an interactive clinical simulation platform that places student nurses inside a real hospital workflow — scanning barcodes, catching allergy conflicts, computing pediatric doses, and responding to patient deterioration — before they ever touch a real patient.
Built around the Barcode Medication Administration (BCMA) system used in actual hospitals, SCAN2BESAFE trains students across four wards — Medical, Surgery, OB, and Pediatrics — through 10 scenario-based cases that mirror genuine clinical situations: an 81-year-old bleeding patient, a preeclamptic mother, a child with a critical drug allergy, a post-op patient in the wrong hands.

Every wrong decision has a consequence. Every right one has a reason.
Students don't just read about medication safety — they experience the moment the allergy alert fires, feel the pressure of a deteriorating patient, and learn why BCMA confirmation alone doesn't mean it's safe to give the drug.

For nursing schools, it's a return demonstration tool that runs 24/7 without consuming clinical hours. For students, it's the safest place to make mistakes — and actually learn from them.

Because the best time to learn what not to do is before the patient is real.
